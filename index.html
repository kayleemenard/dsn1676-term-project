Planets

  • Earth

    The third planet from the Sun, the densest body in our solar system, and the fifth largest. Earth has only one natural satellite.

  • Jupiter

    The fifth planet from the Sun. It's more than twice as large as all the planets of our solar system combined. It has 67 known natural satellites.

  • Mars

    The fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest. Temperatures on the surface can vary from -133°C on a cold night to 27°C on a summer day.

  • Mercury

    The smallest planet since Pluto was demoted, and the closest to the Sun. It is smaller than Saturn's moon, Titan, but it's twice as dense.

  • Neptune

    The furthest from the Sun and the fourth largest. It has the fastest winds in our solar system with can reach 2000km/h.

  • Saturn

    The sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest. It was the first planet to be noted as having rings orbiting around it. It's specific gravity is less than that of water.

  • Uranus

    The seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest. It is pronounced "YOOR-a-nus", not "your anus". It's south pole is almost directly pointing at the Sun.

  • Venus

    The second planet from the Sun and the sixth largest. It is the most circular of all the planets. One day on Venus is the equivalent to 243 Earth days.
